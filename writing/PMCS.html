On the current state of society relative to the title of this page:

As Sir Isaac Newton might have phrased it, "If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants."

I think all advances are made by giants, but that each individual could help push the boundaries; we all have some sort of magnitude. I think it's disappointing we aren't all doing ballet on the peaks.

On quantitative and qualitative results of computational analysis:

Realtive to experiments: You can't know every variable in reality, you have to know every variable to perform computation.

It's likely you can't know every variable you'd like to know to validate your experiment. In making assumptions you can get close and focus on evaluating small error. Concluding a casuse-and-effect chain to explain the error can be difficult to achieve, relative to other worldly things. 100% validation and reductionism is desired but rarely achieved and never communicated broadly. The Unreasonable Effectiveness of Mathematics in the Natural Sciences is on display when classical equations can predict the outcome of solid and fluid mechanic experiments within 5% (easily) via computation. A more complete explanation of where different paradigms of science are validated and where they break down is required in the education system.
