Connecting the Finite and Infinite

back to index

July 6th, 2015

The idea of connecting the finite and infinite is met explicitly when learning limits in calculus; or via alternate branches of mathematics. Typical of most Western education systems this will be viewed through a topic-specific lens instead of philosophically.

Leo Tolstoy presents it in a beautiful form through his A Confession and Other Religious Writings, which I discovered via BrainPickings.org.

The more time you spend with with the concept of connecting the finite and infinite the more you will find it positions itself in thought provoking niches:
- In views of life and religion as commented on by Tolstoy (I would add an individual's contribution to human knowledge, or culture, is analogous in some sense to his presentation of religion).
- Evaluations of possible solutions to quantum gravity and other budding physical theories.
- The foundations of mathematics and computation.

When these (possible) connections are found it is worth pausing to give complete thought.
